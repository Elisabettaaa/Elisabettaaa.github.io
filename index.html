Food Category VS health

What is the distribution of the Nutri-Score within each food category ?

As excepted... the most healthy products are fruits and vegetables ! And obsiously you should avoid to eat snacks and beverages.

Interestingly, the sugary snacks are worst for health than the salty snacks or the fatty products. Indeed the ratio of e products is 49% for sugary snacks and 14% for salty snacks or 22% for fat and sauces. Moreover, the sum of e + d ratios is also higher for sugary snacks.

This could demonstrate that the real ennemy is maybe more sugar than salt or fat which contrast with the standard nutritional recommendations which usually emphasize on fat.

Brands VS Health

Does Carrefour products healthier than Leclerc ones ?

Yes... Unfortunalty, more than 50% of the products sold in France have a bad nutrition grade (d or e). The healthy product tagged with the grade a only represent 13% of the ~100,000 products in the data set. As seen previously, we observe that for almost all the biggest food brands present in France more than 40% of the products are "junk" food (products with labels 'e' or 'd'). We don't eat that well in France...

The exception is 'Picard' in which products tagged with d or e represent one third of the total number of products (which is already quite high...). Actually, Picard mainly sells frozen food which could be excpected to be healthier than the products sold by Netto (cheap product mainly industrial).

For each brand the score will be the average of the ratios for each nutri-score (a,b..) weighted by the score assigned to each nutri-score (5,4..). A score of 5 for a brand means that 100% of the products sold by the brand are a (very healthy).

Bellow is displayed the 10 best (highest scores) and the 10 worst brands (lowest scores) regarding nutrition benefit. As we could expect, eating chocolate is not good... But, suprisingly the 6 worst brands are all chocolate ones except Monin which sell sirop. The best brands are 'D'Aucy' - which sells a lot of vegetables and fruits in cans - and 'Charles et Alice' - which sells fruits in cup.

Palm oil in France

Does french products contain palm oil ?

A large majority (87%) of the products in France don't contain palm oil ! Ok... it can be addressed that a lot of products don't need any oil (palm or not!). For all the brands more than 70% of the products does NOT contain palm oil.
  • However, 27% of the products made by 'Leclerc' contain or may contain palm oil. It is a high ratio!
  • Picard and Monoprix are the more "environmental" brand regarding palm oil presence in their products.
