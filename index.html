Food Categorization

Let's start looking at the dataset and how we decide to analyse it.

The data set we analysed comes from the Open Food Facts database that gathers information and data on food products from around the world and it gets constantly update. The data set includes almost 1 GB of data, but more than half is rappresented by null or missing values.

In our analysis, we decide to consider seven different food categories, getting rid of their null products: Bevarages, Cereal and Potatoes, Composite Foods,Fat and Souces, Fish and Meats and Eggs, Fruits and Vegetables, Milk and daity products, Salty Snacks and Sugary snacks. In the table below there is an example of how we divide the different products according the above cited categories.

Nutritional Values For Category

Which nutritional value do we care ?

The goal of our project is to give the consumer advices about the healthiness of the products for the different categories according to a specific score. We decide to create our own score that includes three features available in the data set: Nutritional score, Energy over 100g and number of oil palm ingredients.
In order to fill the missing data for the nutritional score and the product categories, we decided to use a Random Forest Classifier based on the following features: energy, fat sugars proteins carbohydrates sodium and salt for 100 g of products.

Food Category VS health

What is the distribution of the Nutri-Score within each food category ?

As excepted... the most healthy products are fruits and vegetables ! And obsiously you should avoid to eat snacks and beverages.

Interestingly, the sugary snacks are worst for health than the salty snacks or the fatty products. Indeed the ratio of e products is 49% for sugary snacks and 14% for salty snacks or 22% for fat and sauces.Moreover, the sum of e + d ratios is also higher for sugary snacks.

This could demonstrate that the real ennemy is maybe more sugar than salt or fat which contrast with the standard nutritional recommendations which usually emphasize on fat.
